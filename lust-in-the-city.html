Chapter Two: Wine and Whispers

It was a Wednesday night—June's favourite kind. A night to herself. No noise, no obligations. Just soft lights, her ridiculous but therapeutic skincare routine, and that feel good kind of silence that only came when you lived alone downtown.

She’d just smeared on her green clay face mask—the one that made her look like Princess Fiona from Shrek—and slipped under her duvet with a glass of wine, ready to binge her comfort series on Netflix, when her phone buzzed.

FaceTime. Alex.

She froze. Then sighed. “Of course.”

She answered. “Why are you calling me at almost midnight?”

Alex was glowing through the screen, carefree. She had something funny to share—some chaotic story from earlier—and as she laughed, June couldn’t help noticing… Alex wasn’t wearing a bra.

Her nipples pressed gently against her tank top, barely visible but absolutely there. And once June noticed, it was impossible to unsee. Her wine suddenly felt warmer. Her mouth, drier.

Then came the question. Quiet. Straightforward.

“Can you come over?”

June blinked. “What?”

“I don’t want to be alone tonight,” Alex said, softly. “I want… company. Yours.”

June hesitated. Looked at the clock. Almost 12:30 a.m.

She hung up.

Not in rejection—but in motion. She peeled off her mask, threw on a soft dress, and this time—she didn’t call a cab.

She grabbed her car keys. She didn’t feel safe with strange men driving her anymore. Not when she was vulnerable. Not tonight.

The drive was quiet. Her heart was louder than the music.

Alex opened the door almost instantly, like she’d been waiting behind it the whole time.

The lights were low. Candles flickered. A jazz record whispered through the flat. And there—on the table—another bottle of wine, already breathing.

“You came,” Alex said, smiling.

“You said you’d wait.”

They didn’t rush. They talked. Laughed. Let the candles burn lower.

And eventually, when Alex leaned in and kissed her—it wasn’t urgent. It was soft. Earnest. Their lips met slowly, like a song with no chorus, just harmony.

They undressed each other like they were learning the story by touch.

Every motion. Every sigh. Every look—it all felt new. Tender. Intentional.

When June came—it was with her eyes open. With Alex watching her. With her hands holding her. With the music still playing low behind them and the candles flickering against the walls.

They lay there. Tangled. Breathing. Satisfied but not finished.

Alex pulled her close, lips brushing her temple. “This is what I meant when I said I didn’t want to be alone tonight.”

June smiled against her skin. “Good. Because I’m not leaving anytime soon.”
