My Story

I come from a family of tailors. My aunt was a local champion—she made clothes for everyone in our community: chiefs, their wives, maidens, the young and the old. I remember Christmas Eves with children lined up, excited for their new outfits. That joy—that atmosphere—sparked something in me.

I got my first sewing machine at 14. From making outfits for myself and my siblings, I started designing for friends and neighbours. I later became a model and met amazing fashion designers who inspired me deeply. Over the past decade, I’ve styled brides, celebrities, and royalty. Fashion is more than craft to me—it’s a calling.

And with that calling came a mission: to promote African fashion unapologetically. I tell stories through fabric, silhouette, and colour—stories rooted in culture, boldness, and pride. Today, I’m joining a movement of a thousand African creatives with one mission: ✨ To put Africa on the global stage, one outfit at a time.
